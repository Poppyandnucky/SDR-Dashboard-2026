================================================================================
MASTER PROMPT — Generate stakeholder UI mockups (SDR Dashboard, Approach B)
================================================================================
Use this prompt with an image/wireframe AI (Midjourney, DALL·E, Figma AI, etc.)
or a slide-design AI. Repeat per screen, or ask for a "8-screen user flow"
wireframe set in one style.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONTEXT (paste verbatim)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We are replacing a Streamlit-based policy dashboard with a new web experience.
Backend stays Python (simulation API). Users are NON-TECHNICAL: program managers,
policymakers, funders. They must configure scenarios without percentages in the
main flow — only Low / Medium / High intensity per domain (Demand, Supply,
Quality, Transport), mapped to numbers server-side. Navigation is a LINEAR WIZARD
(4 steps) with a visible progress indicator, not nested expanders. Results show a
KPI strip, one primary "baseline vs scenario" time chart, then accordions for
maternal, neonatal, facility outcomes. Aesthetic: calm, trustworthy healthcare
analytics — plenty of white space, large type, WCAG-friendly contrast, no
cluttered dashboards. Optional collapsed "Advanced — for analysts" section.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GLOBAL STYLE (all screens)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Device: desktop web app, 1440px wide, generous margins.
- Typography: modern sans (Inter or similar); H1 32–40px, body 16–18px.
- Color: white/off-white background; primary blue #2c529e; accent teal or soft
  green for "positive change"; muted red only for "worse than baseline" deltas.
- Components: rounded cards (12–16px radius), subtle shadow, clear primary button
  (filled blue), secondary outline button.
- Accessibility: visible focus rings, labels on all controls, no color-only meaning.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCREEN-BY-SCREEN (ask the AI to produce one image or one slide each)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1) WELCOME / LANDING
   - Hero: "SDR Outcomes Explorer" (or similar) + one-line subtitle about
     comparing maternal and neonatal intervention scenarios for planning.
   - Illustration: abstract map or gentle medical line-art (not charts).
   - CTA: large "Start scenario" button. Secondary text link "How this works".

2) CHOOSE FOCUS (step 1 of 4)
   - Progress: "Step 1 of 4 — Your focus".
   - Four equal-width CARDS in a row (or 2x2 on smaller width), each with icon,
     bold title, two lines of plain language. Titles example: "Health system
     strengthening", "Clinical & diagnosis packages", "Combined program",
     "Community & digital programs". One card has a selected state (blue border).

3) INTENSITY — LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH (step 2 of 4)
   - Progress: "Step 2 of 4 — How strong is the program?"
   - For 3–4 rows, each row has a domain label (Demand, Supply, Quality,
     Referral & transport) and a SEGMENTED CONTROL with three pills: Low |
     Medium | High. No percentage sliders visible.
   - Small helper text under one row as example: "Higher demand means more women
     aim for ANC and facility delivery — exact numbers are set by the model."
   - Footer: Back | Continue.

4) PATH-SPECIFIC OPTIONS (step 3 of 4) — optional screen
   - Show only if "Combined" or "Community" was chosen: simple toggles with
     labels "PROMPTS", "Mentors", "PULSE" with short descriptions; each optional
     sub-area uses Low/Med/High for "reach" not percentages.
   - Progress bar at top. Back | Continue.

5) RUN MODEL (step 4 of 4)
   - Time horizon: three large radio tiles "Short (~1 year)", "Medium (~3 years)",
     "Long (~5 years)".
   - Toggle: "Show uncertainty band (multiple model runs)" — off by default.
   - Optional second scenario: collapsed "Compare two programs" with short explainer.
   - Primary button full width: "Run model" with rocket or play icon.

6) RESULTS — HEADLINE
   - Top: row of 4 KPI tiles — large number, small label, delta vs baseline
     (e.g. "−12% vs baseline") with micro-copy "lower is better" where relevant.
   - Below: ONE wide chart — two lines (Baseline vs Your scenario) over months,
     clean legend, grid subtle.
   - Sticky subnav or tabs: "Overview" (selected) | "Explore outcomes".

7) EXPLORE OUTCOMES
   - Left sidebar or accordion: Maternal | Neonatal | Facility & capacity.
   - Main area: one chart with shaded confidence band (median line), title clear.
   - Breadcrumb or "Back to overview".

8) ADVANCED (collapsed)
   - Show a screen mock where an accordion is CLOSED, labeled "Advanced — for
     analysts" with a small warning icon and one line of text. Suggests sliders
     inside without drawing all sliders.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NEGATIVE PROMPT / AVOID
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
No dense Excel-like grids, no tiny Streamlit widgets, no walls of acronyms
without expansion, no dark hacker terminal aesthetic, no 3D glossy charts,
no cluttered "enterprise dashboard" with 20 charts on one page.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT REQUEST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Produce [wireframe | high-fidelity mockup | slide deck layout] in [style name].
Consistent component library across all screens. Include empty states where
relevant (before run: "Run the model to see results" in the results layout).
